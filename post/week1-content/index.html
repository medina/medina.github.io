It’s a goal to “spell it all out” in this course.

Don’t let any acronym go unexpanded, or new term go undefined.

When in doubt, ask!

  • https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Cuckoo’s_Egg, with the sub-title “Tracking a Spy Through the Maze of Computer Espionage”
  • https://www.owasp.org/index.php/Builders / https://www.owasp.org/index.php/Breakers / https://www.owasp.org/index.php/Defenders - labels OWASP assigns to different security communities
  • https://www.pcisecuritystandards.org/ is the https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Payment_Card_Industry_Data_Security_Standard. Remember, every retailer was compliant, until they weren’t!
  • https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/EMV - smartcard (something you have) and a PIN (something you know), common in Europe, but not the US.
  • CISO / CSO: Chief Information Security Officer or Chief Security Officer. Or “Chief Sacrificial Offering” when something goes wrong. (See https://www.nytimes.com/2014/07/21/business/a-tough-corporate-job-asks-one-question-can-you-hack-it.html
  • CIO: Chief Information Officer
  • Risk assessment, acceptance, remediation: some quantitative assessments may take the form likelihood x frequency x impact; we can remediate risk to obtain some residual of the originally calculated risk (by deploying counter-measures for a known threat, for example); the remaining risk may be accepted to obtain a larger goal, such as new business, cost avoidance, etc.
  • Don’t forget reputational damage when calculating impact. As Ben Franklin said, “It takes many good deeds to build a good reputation, and only one bad one to lose it.”
  • Various models of the http://www.eur.army.mil/vigilance/Cyber_Attack_Cycle.pdf, with phases such as Reconnaissance, Exploit, and repeating the sequence to move laterally as needed.
  • https://www.cert.org/insider-threat/ is real, and there are numerous techniques for detecting http://www.sei.cmu.edu/reports/12tr012.pdf that will also help when your own users’s credentials are being used by an adversary.
  • https://www.wired.com/insights/2013/02/identity-the-new-security-perimeter/ - because once you have authorized credentials, you can get through all those pesky firewalls, computer logins, etc.
  • A classmates quips: “Security, Performance, Usability: Pick One”, a good joke on the old “Good, Fast, Cheap: Pick Two” triad.
